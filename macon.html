The College Hill Corridor

A two-square mile area that elegantly connects Mercer's campus to downtown Macon's business and cultural core. Tree-lined streets and a mix of historic homes create a neighborhood fabric that supports both long-term residents and the transient energy of university life.

Historic Architecture

Craftsman bungalows and cottages built between 1890 and 1930. These homes represent a period of architectural confidence and craftsmanship that continues to attract those who appreciate authenticity over new construction.

Music Heritage

Birthplace of Southern Rock and Soul

There are three M's in the history of rock & roll — Memphis, Muscle Shoals, and Macon.
— Bonnie Bramlett, rock and soul vocalist

It's impossible to explain how little Macon, Georgia, became an epicenter of influence on modern pop music. Aside from Memphis and Detroit, it would be difficult to find one city that has produced more groundbreaking musical figures in the 20th century.

Little Richard

The Architect of Rock & Roll

Born Richard Wayne Penniman in Macon in 1932. One of the original inductees into the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame. His explosive performances and iconic songs like "Tutti Frutti" and "Long Tall Sally" shaped the sound of rock music forever.

Visit Little Richard House →

Otis Redding

King of Soul

Grew up in Macon, dropped out at 15 to work with Little Richard's backing band, the Upsetters. "I would not be here without Richard — he is my inspiration." His "(Sittin' On) The Dock of the Bay" became the first posthumous #1 single.

Otis Redding Foundation →

The Allman Brothers Band

Southern Rock Pioneers

Signed with Phil Walden's Capricorn Records in 1969 and made Macon their home. Lived communally at "The Big House," rehearsed constantly, and found songwriting inspiration in Rose Hill Cemetery.

The Big House Museum →

Capricorn Records

1969-1979: The Southern Rock Era

Founded by Phil Walden, who managed Otis Redding, Percy Sledge, and Sam & Dave. Between 1969 and 1979, produced 9 platinum albums, 17 gold albums, and 5 gold singles. Launched the Allman Brothers, Wet Willie, and Marshall Tucker Band.

Mercer Music at Capricorn →

Historic & Live Music Venues

Grant's Lounge

Original Home of Southern Rock • Est. 1971

Three blocks from Capricorn Studios, Grant's hosted all Capricorn artists for legendary jam sessions. Tom Petty, Eric Clapton, and the Marshall Tucker Band all played here. Live music Wed-Sun with open jams on Sunday.

The Douglass Theatre

Historic Venue • Est. 1921

Founded by Charles H. Douglass, Macon's first African American millionaire. Hosted Bessie Smith, Ma Rainey, Duke Ellington, and Cab Calloway. Young Otis Redding performed here on the "Teenage Party" talent show.

Capricorn Sound Studios

Recording Studio & Museum

Now "Mercer Music at Capricorn" — the fully restored studio offers recording facilities, a music incubator for young musicians, and an interpretive center with historic artifacts and interactive exhibits.

The Hummingbird Stage & Taproom

Live Music Venue

A beloved live music venue in the heart of downtown Macon. Features touring bands and local artists across rock, country, blues, and Americana genres.

JBA

Jazz Bar • 425 Cherry St

Intimate music venue featuring live jazz, jam nights, dart tournaments, and karaoke. Craft cocktails with fresh house-made ingredients and an extensive whiskey selection.

Otis Redding Center for the Arts

Opening 2025

Fulfills Redding's dream of music education for Macon's children. Located near his statue at Gateway Park. Will offer programs for aspiring musicians as young as 4 years old.

🏛️ Museums & Culture

From ancient mounds to African American heritage — Macon's cultural institutions are world-class.

Tubman Museum

African American History

The largest museum in the Southeast devoted to African American art, history and culture. 49,500 sq ft facility with galleries including "From Africa to America."

Ocmulgee Mounds National Historical Park

National Park • 17,000 Years

Earth lodge with original floors from 1010 AD. Walk atop the Great Temple Mound for views of downtown Macon. One of the oldest continuously occupied sites in North America.

The Big House Museum

Allman Brothers Band

The communal home where the Allman Brothers Band lived from 1970-1973. Features personal artifacts, instruments, and memorabilia from the Southern rock pioneers.

Hay House

Historic Mansion • 1859

An 18,000 sq ft Italian Renaissance Revival mansion. Called the "Palace of the South" — features period rooms, secret passages, and stunning architecture.

Georgia Sports Hall of Fame

Sports History

43,000 sq ft museum honoring Georgia's greatest athletes. Interactive exhibits and memorabilia from legends across all sports.

Museum of Arts and Sciences

Art • Science • Planetarium

Middle Georgia's museum of discovery featuring art galleries, science exhibits, a planetarium, and nature trails. Great for families.
